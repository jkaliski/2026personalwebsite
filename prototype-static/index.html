§ I — Biography

A musician of many rooms.

From the Polish piano tradition to the opera stage, by way of the German lied and the baroque continuo bench.

Trained first as a concert pianist at the F. Nowowiejski Academy in Bydgoszcz under Prof. Katarzyna Popowa-Zydroń — whose Chopin lineage he still carries into the rehearsal room — Jarosław Kaliski moved to Stockholm in 2009 for postgraduate studies at the Royal College of Music, and then to Detmold, Germany, where he completed a Master in Liedgestaltung with distinction under Prof. Manuel Lange.

The through-line is language. Whether on the modern grand, the fortepiano, or the harpsichord, his work centres on how a text sings: the phonetic weight of a consonant, the breath a diphthong asks for, the historical ornament that turns a declamation into music. He has coached German for Marlis Petersen's Marietta in Mariusz Treliński's Die tote Stadt, realised continuo for Jean-Christophe Spinosi's La Cenerentola at the Royal Swedish Opera, and tuned and played harpsichords through productions from Lully to Händel to Mozart.

Since 2021 he has been Assistant Professor of Musical Performance in Opera at Stockholm University of the Arts, where he teaches German Lyric Diction, co-leads the annual Early Music Seminar, and mentors young singers through their first complete operatic roles. His pedagogical research — the online course series Lyric LAB — is now used by over a hundred Scandinavian singers as part of the standard curriculum in both Italian, French and German diction.

In summer 2026 he joins the musical team of Confidencen Opera & Music Festival's Female Edition — Francesca Caccini's La liberazione di Ruggiero dall'isola d'Alcina, directed by Jacopo Spirei under the musical direction of Peter Spissky.

Confidencen Opera & Music Festival · Female Edition

La liberazione di Ruggiero
dall'isola d'Alcina

Francesca Caccini (1625) · libretto by Ferdinando Saracinelli

A tale of power, magic and seductive illusion — the knight Ruggiero held on Alcina's island, where reality bends to her will, until the sorceress Melissa comes, disguised as his old mentor, to break the spell. A duel between two formidable women; an allegory of liberation that leaves a long shadow.

“La Liberazione is much more than a traditional story of liberation. What fascinates me is the intense struggle between Alcina and Melissa — two women who shape reality in wholly different ways.”
— Jacopo Spirei, director

The production closes with a composed horse ballet for twenty-four pairs, staged inside Confidencen — a theatre built in the 1670s as a royal riding house. A co-production with Den Andra Operan.

§ VI — Pedagogy

Lyric LAB.

An online lyric-diction curriculum for Swedish singers — part of the course material at SKH Opera. Interactive modules, native-speaker audio, IPA, and the repertoire to practise on.
I.

Italian

Launched 2017 · 100+ students

Six modules from Latin roots through seven-vowel modifications, gemination, syllabification, and the poetic meters of Mozart's recitatives. Developed with Mia Rosetti.

II.

French

Launched 2019

Nasal vowels, the e muet, liaison and the particular architecture of French mélodie — developed with Swedish-French baritone Eric Schoeffler.

III.

German

Launched 2024 · SKH Opera curriculum

Seven modules from the Germanic roots of Hochdeutsch through Auslautverhärtung, umlauts, and stage pronunciation. Developed with soprano Elisabeth Meyer.

Student · Norwegian Academy of Music
“The course demonstrates clearly and concretely how to physically form the different vowel and consonant sounds. I've had several aha moments during the course and got answers to things I didn't even know I was wondering about.”
— Student testimonial, lyriclab.se
